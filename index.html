What does skipping a day do to your workload?

A semester is a finite, surprisingly small number of work-days, and the term's work is split across them. Skip one and that work doesn't disappear — it lands on the days that are left. Skip a few more and the pile on each remaining day grows faster and faster, because there are fewer days each time to absorb it. Set up your term below, toggle the days you might miss, and see how quickly "I'll catch up later" stops being realistic.

The curve is hyperbolic, not a straight line — each extra skip hurts more than the last, because the days left to absorb the work keep shrinking. And there's a practical limit on how much you can pile onto a single day: 8 hours is already a heavy day, and a sustained 10 is about as much genuinely demanding work as almost anyone can do well. Past that 10-hour ceiling a few people can still grind it out — but for most students what's really at stake isn't whether the work technically fits, it's your top grades.
